Let's figure out in which area of software development you fit.

Time Task
Start of the day A software developer's day typically starts with a morning routine, often including a cup of coffee and reviewing emails or messages to catch up on any updates.
Meeting They then attend a stand-up meeting, where team members quickly share progress, plans, and blockers.
Coding After the meeting, the developer dives into coding, tackling tasks from their project board. This involves writing new code, debugging, or performing code reviews. Problem-solving is a significant part of the job, requiring both concentration and creativity.
Lunch Lunch is often a welcome break, offering a chance to recharge.
Afternoon In the afternoon, developers may continue coding, collaborate with colleagues to discuss solutions, or attend additional meetings related to project planning or technical discussions.
End of the day As the day winds down, they often document their work, update task boards, and plan the next steps for upcoming tasks. After ensuring that no critical issues remain, the developer logs off, concluding another day of creating and refining software.
